MutthaSales Enterprise Platform

Role: Product Engineer · Data Analyst · Dashboard Architect

B2B/B2C Operations KPI Intelligence Role-Based Workflow Analytics-Driven Decisions

Executive Summary

The MutthaSales platform was designed as an internal growth engine to unify sales, operations, and analytics for faster decision-making. Instead of fragmented spreadsheets and delayed reports, teams get a shared workspace with near real-time visibility into pipeline, order flow, and channel performance.

Business Context

Before Platform

  • Teams used disconnected tools and manually merged reports.
  • Leadership lacked a single, trusted dashboard for decision cycles.
  • Weekly reviews were delayed by data cleanup and reconciliation.

After Platform Direction

  • Centralized analytics and operational workflows in one product.
  • Role-based visibility for managers, analysts, and operations.
  • Standard KPI definitions to reduce reporting ambiguity.

Problem

Business operations and channel metrics were spread across multiple tools, creating delayed decisions and inconsistent reporting.

Objectives & Success Criteria

1 Source

Build one reliable source of truth for commercial and operational metrics.

Faster Reviews

Reduce weekly reporting preparation overhead through built-in dashboards.

Role Clarity

Give each function targeted views to act without cross-team dependencies.

Approach

  • Designed a unified SaaS workflow for teams and leadership.
  • Built KPI dashboards and reporting modules for operational clarity.
  • Structured data views for e-commerce and distribution actions.
  • Defined metric governance (naming, formulas, cadence) for consistency.
  • Prioritized mobile-friendly navigation for on-ground business stakeholders.

Platform Architecture (High-Level)

  • Ingestion Layer: Consolidates transactions and channel updates into a normalized reporting model.
  • Business Logic Layer: Applies KPI formulas, status tagging, and role-based filtering.
  • Presentation Layer: Delivers operational dashboards, trend snapshots, and drill-down reporting.
  • Action Layer: Enables follow-up workflows from insights (e.g., review, correction, escalation).

Delivery Timeline

  1. Discovery: Mapped reporting pain points and decision bottlenecks across teams.
  2. MVP Definition: Prioritized essential dashboards and role-level workflows.
  3. Build & Validate: Implemented core modules and validated KPI parity with legacy reports.
  4. Adoption: Structured team onboarding and iterative refinement from stakeholder feedback.

Impact

  • Created a single source of truth for business performance.
  • Improved operational visibility across roles and departments.
  • Enabled faster decisions through centralized KPI monitoring.
  • Reduced ambiguity in business reviews with standardized KPI definitions.
  • Improved accountability with role-specific access and ownership views.

Key Challenges & How I Solved Them

Data Consistency

Different teams interpreted the same metric differently. I introduced shared KPI definitions and formula alignment checkpoints before release.

Stakeholder Adoption

Users initially relied on legacy sheets. I used phased onboarding and familiar dashboard patterns to reduce switching friction.

Cross-Function Priorities

Operational and managerial priorities differed. I organized views by role while preserving a consistent global data model.

Decision Latency

Insights came too late in review cycles. I prioritized alert-oriented summaries and trend visibility for faster interventions.
